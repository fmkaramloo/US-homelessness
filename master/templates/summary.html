Overview

More than thousands of people are sleeping on streets and homeless shelters around the bay area. The purpose of this project is to explore the homeless population in California. The homeless population dataset gathered include population count by county and by city in multiple years. The project includes visualizations showing the severity of homelessness in the entire state of California over the years.

Conclusion

Based on the quantitative datasets gathered, the overall total homeless population in the United States slightly decreased from year 2010 to 2019 by approximately 11 percent. Despite the decrease in total number, homelessness continues to be a significant issue in some states. California remains as the state with the most homeless population from 2010 to 2019 followed by New York and Florida. The overall homeless population in California shows a significant increase over a decade. Wyoming remains as the state with the lowest homeless population.
